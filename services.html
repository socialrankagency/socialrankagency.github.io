Common Questions

Frequently asked questions about our services

Still deciding what you need? Here's what most clients ask before we start.

Do you guarantee a #1 Google ranking?+

No honest agency can guarantee a specific position — Google's algorithm is outside anyone's control. What we guarantee is a proper, transparent process: technical fixes, content strategy and consistent reporting that moves rankings in the right direction.

Is SEO a one-time job or ongoing work?+

SEO is ongoing. Search engines and competitors keep changing, so rankings need continuous monitoring, content updates and technical maintenance to hold their position — not just climb once.

Do you handle local SEO for a specific city?+

Yes — Google Business Profile setup, local citations and location-targeted keywords are all part of our SEO service, whether you're targeting one city or multiple regions.

Will my website be built on a page builder like WordPress?+

By default we hand-code every site in HTML, CSS and JavaScript for speed and control. We also work within WordPress, Shopify or other platforms if that better suits your budget or ongoing editing needs.

Can you redesign my existing website instead of building from scratch?+

Yes. We can rebuild an existing site while keeping your branding and content, or design something completely new — whichever gets you the best result.

Do you build e-commerce or booking websites?+

Yes — online stores, appointment booking and payment integrations are all part of our website development service.

Which platforms do you run ads and campaigns on?+

Mainly Meta (Facebook & Instagram) and Google Ads, alongside organic social content — we'll recommend the mix that fits your budget and where your customers actually are.

Do I need a website before starting digital marketing?+

It helps, but it's not required — we can run social media and ad campaigns pointing to a landing page, WhatsApp or Google Business Profile while your full website is being built.

How is digital marketing performance reported?+

You get monthly reports covering reach, engagement, leads and ad spend — plain-language summaries, not raw dashboards you have to decode yourself.
